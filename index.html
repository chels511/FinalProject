DESENSITIZATION

THE CHILD OF PSYCHOLOGY

Mary Cover Jones

Referred to as "the mother of behavior therapy" for her graduate work developing and testing techniques to reduce or eliminate phobias in children by introducing a series of stimuli similar to what they are afraid of until they get to the actual phobic stimulus.

Joseph Wolpe

Developed systematic densitization- a behavior therapy that occurs in three steps.The first step is to identify the hierarchy of fears. The second step is to learn relaxation or coping techniques. Finally, the individual uses these techniques to manage their fear during a situation from the hierarchy.

Albert Bandura

Originatior of the social learning theory- the concept of a learning process which proposes that new behaviors can be acquired by observing and imitating others and for the 1961 Bobo doll experiment that demonstrated children imitating physical violence from an adult.

Clark Leonard Hull

Created the drive theory- stating that no learning occurred unless a drive produced tension and impelled the organism into activity to procure a reward that would reduce the drive and satisfy its related physiological need.

TYPES

Do you associate with any of them? Check Below!

Sexual Desensitization

Sexual desensitization from pornography refers to a phenomenon where individuals, after prolonged exposure to explicit sexual content, may experience a diminished response or sensitivity to sexual stimuli. Research indicates that porn consumers can become desensitized to porn, often needing to consume more porn, more extreme forms of porn, or consume porn more often in order to get the same response they once did.

Emotional/Empathy Desensitization

Overexposure to emotionally charged content, such as tragic news stories or dramatic portrayals of suffering, may desensitize individuals to feelings of empathy or emotional resonance. This could result in decreased emotional reactions or concern for others' distress.

Social Media Desensitization

Constant exposure to curated or exaggerated portrayals of lifestyles, achievements, or body image on social media platforms can desensitize individuals to reality, leading to feelings of inadequacy, comparison, or desensitization to others' emotions or experiences.

Violence Desensitization

Exposure to violent content in movies, video games, or news media can desensitize individuals to violence. This might lead to decreased emotional reactions or empathy toward violent acts, making it seem more acceptable or less shocking. Research also belives it may be incorporated into our actions and behavior.

Sensory Densensitization

Continuous exposure to certain sensory experiences in media, such as loud noises, bright flashing lights, or intense visual effects in movies or video games, can desensitize individuals to those specific sensory stimuli. Over time, this may reduce the sensitivity or responsiveness to such sensory input.

Risk Densensitization

Exposure to media representations of risky behaviors, extreme stunts, or dangerous activities (e.g., in movies, reality shows, social media) might desensitize individuals to the associated risks. This could lead to underestimating the dangers or consequences of such behaviors in real life.
